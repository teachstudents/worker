🗣️ Pronunciation: Weak vs. Strong Forms

Using weak and strong forms correctly helps you sound more fluent and native-like.

Weak Forms (弱读形式)

In normal speech, small function words like prepositions (to, of, at, from, for) are usually unstressed and pronounced using their weak forms.

  • "at" becomes /ət/ — "We met at uni" (我们在大学认识)
  • "for" becomes /fə/ — "I've known him for ten years" (我认识他十年了)

Strong Forms (重读形式)

Even in fast speech, you must use the strong, stressed form of a word in three specific situations:

  1. If you want to emphasise or contrast it
    (e.g., "The present's not from Mark, it's for Mark" (礼物不是马克送的，是给马克的)).
  2. If it comes before a pause.
  3. If it comes at the end of a sentence
    (e.g., "What are you looking at?" (你在看什么？)).

Note: Other function words like positive auxiliary verbs (can, must), pronouns (you, them), conjunctions (and, but), and articles also follow these weak/strong pronunciation rules.

📝 Model Answers: Family Matters & Friends

Part 1: Tell me something about your family. Who are you close to?

"Although I'm quite busy with my high school studies, I always make time for my family. We are quite close-knit. I am particularly close to my older brother. He helps me a lot with my difficult homework assignments, and we often play video games together on weekends."

Part 2: Describe a close friend. (How long you've known them, how you met, what kind of person they are).

"I’d like to talk about my close friend, Daniel, someone I’ve known for almost fifteen years. We first met when we were in middle school. At that time, we were both quite ambitious and joined the same English club, so we naturally spent a lot of time together. What really brought us closer was a group project where we had to design a small magazine. I remember staying up late at his house, drinking cheap soda, and laughing at our terrible drawings. Since then, we’ve stayed in touch and our friendship has grown stronger year by year. In terms of personality, Daniel is honestly one of the most reliable people I’ve ever met. If he promises to do something, he will definitely follow through. He’s also extremely hard‑working; even when we were teenagers, he was the kind of person who would finish his homework before playing games. At the same time, he’s very open‑minded, which makes him easy to talk to. Actually, he’s the opposite of judgemental; he listens first and gives thoughtful advice later. Another thing I appreciate is that he’s quite outgoing and sociable. I like him mainly because he’s self‑assured without being arrogant. He knows who he is and what he wants, and that confidence inspires me. Whenever I face a difficult decision, he’s the first person I call. So overall, Daniel is a close friend I deeply value, not only because of his personality, but because of the shared experiences that connect us."

Part 3: In what ways have families changed in the last hundred years? Do you think friendships change as we get older?

"I would argue that families spend significantly less time together nowadays. Students are overwhelmed with homework, and parents work long hours. Regarding friendships, I definitely believe they evolve. When you are younger, you bond over simple hobbies, but as adults, you tend to value friends who are reliable and open-minded, rather than just sociable."

💪 Health & Diet Vocabulary

  • Sports: contact sports, water sports, extreme sports, team sports, and individual sports.
  • Health and diet: malnourished (营养不良), metabolism (新陈代谢), carbohydrates / vitamins / calories (碳水化合物 / 维生素 / 卡路里), crash diet (节食速瘦法), put on weight, lose weight, obese, and underweight (肥胖 / 过瘦).

The two distinct meanings of "diet":

  1. The type and range of food you regularly eat (e.g., "I have a balanced diet" or "I have quite a poor diet"). (日常饮食)
  2. Eating special food or less food to lose weight (e.g., "I plan to go on a diet"). (节食)

🔗 Vocabulary: Collocations

Collocations are words that naturally group together, and using them correctly will make your English sound much more natural. Instead of learning isolated words, you should learn these word combinations:

  • Verb + adjective: keep fit (保持健康), keep active (保持活跃).
  • Verb + noun: play volleyball (打排球).
  • Verb + adjective + noun: set a good example (树立好榜样), lead healthy lives (过健康的生活).
  • Noun + noun: form of exercise (锻炼方式), sense of achievement (成就感).
  • Adjective + noun: balanced diet (均衡饮食), passive smoking (吸二手烟), public places (公共场所).

🗣️ Expressing Opinions

To express your views clearly and achieve a higher mark in the Speaking exam, you should use a wide range of phrases instead of always relying on basic phrases like "I think".

  • For basic personal opinions: Use phrases like "If you ask me,...", "In my view,...", "I would argue that...", or "I would say...". (表达基本个人观点)
  • For controversial topics (especially in Part 3): Use stronger phrases such as "I (strongly) believe that...", "As far as I'm concerned,...", "I'm (strongly) in favour of / against...", "I'm convinced / not convinced that would work because...", "I'm sceptical of the idea that...", or "I must admit, I think...". (表达对于争议话题的强烈观点)

Example: "I'm strongly in favour of making all children do at least one hour of sport a day" to combat obesity.

"Yes, definitely. Teenagers today lead a much more sedentary lifestyle because of video games, social media, and endless hours studying at a desk. Instead, schools should set a good example by serving a balanced diet in the cafeteria and encouraging daily physical activity."

    🎯 Exam Technique: Coherence

    Coherence involves ordering points logically using 'signposting' (Firstly, Secondly, Last but not least). Connect opposing ideas with On the one hand... But then again. Use pronouns (he, she, they, it) to avoid repetitive nouns.

    "I strongly agree that travel broadens the mind, as it forces you out of your comfort zone and exposes you to different ways of living. However, massive influxes of tourists can put a severe strain on local infrastructure and lead to environmental damage, such as footpath erosion and littering in previously pristine areas."

    📺 Media & Entertainment Vocabulary

    • Media habits: binge-watch (追剧), prime-time (黄金时段), scrolling (刷手机屏幕), subscribe (订阅).
    • Internet content: biased (有偏见的), viral (病毒式传播的), spoiler (剧透), clickbait (标题党), influencers (网红).

    🗣️ Grammar & Pronunciation

    • Intonation for Surprise: When discussing shocking news or plot twists, let your pitch rise and fall significantly to sound engaged.
    • Adverbs of Degree: Enhance your descriptions with adverbs like absolutely, completely, utterly (e.g., "The movie's ending was completely unpredictable").

    🎯 Exam Technique: Expanding on Habits

    Instead of just stating what you watch, always explain why. Discussing how media helps you unwind from school stress adds depth to your answer.

    📝 Model Answers: Media & Entertainment

    Part 1: Do you prefer watching TV or reading? What kind of programs do you like?

    "After a long, hectic day of high school classes, I usually prefer binge-watching comedy series on my tablet to unwind. I find that humorous shows are a great way to relieve academic stress without requiring too much mental effort."

    Part 2: Describe a movie or TV show you recently watched.

    "I'd like to talk about a sci-fi movie I watched last weekend with my classmates. We had just finished our midterm exams, so we went to the local cinema to celebrate. The movie was about space exploration, and the visual effects were absolutely state-of-the-art. I loved it because the plot was incredibly original and kept us on the edge of our seats. We spent the whole bus ride home discussing the unpredictable ending."

    Part 3: Do teenagers spend too much time on screens? How does social media affect the news?

    "I strongly believe that excessive screen time is a major issue for my generation. Many teenagers follow online influencers and get distracted by viral videos instead of doing their coursework. Furthermore, social media often spreads clickbait and biased news, which makes it difficult for young people to find reliable information."

    🛍️ Shopping & Money Vocabulary

    • Shopping habits: window shopping (只逛不买), impulse buy (冲动消费), brand-conscious (追求名牌的), bargain hunting (寻找便宜货).
    • Money management: pocket money / allowance (零花钱), rip-off (敲竹杠), budget (预算), save up (攒钱).

    🗣️ Grammar & Pronunciation

    • Second Conditional: Use this to talk about imaginary spending: "If I had more pocket money, I would buy a new laptop."
    • Linking 'r': In British and Australian English, pronounce the 'r' at the end of a word if the next word begins with a vowel (e.g., far away, four apples).

    🎯 Exam Technique: Personal vs. General

    In Part 1, talk strictly about your own habits (e.g., your allowance). In Part 3, shift to general society (e.g., consumerism, global economy).

    📝 Model Answers: Shopping & Money

    Part 1: Do you enjoy shopping? Who do you usually go shopping with?

    "I really enjoy window shopping with my friends at the local mall on weekends. However, since I only get a small amount of pocket money from my parents, I rarely make impulse buys and prefer to save up for things I genuinely need."

    Part 2: Describe something you bought recently that you are happy with.

    "Recently, I bought a new, heavy-duty backpack for school. I had been bargain hunting online for weeks because I wanted something durable but affordable. I finally found one on sale. I am extremely happy with it because it has special compartments for my laptop and textbooks, making my daily commute to high school much more organized and comfortable."

    Part 3: Should high school students be taught financial management? Does advertising manipulate consumers?

    "Absolutely. Learning how to budget an allowance and avoid rip-offs are essential life skills that teenagers need before moving away for university. Regarding advertising, I do think it can be manipulative. Many brands use celebrity endorsements to make teenagers feel overly brand-conscious, pressuring them into buying expensive items to fit in."

    ⏰ Daily Routine Vocabulary

    • Time management: hectic (忙碌的), procrastinate (拖延), prioritize (优先考虑), downtime (休息时间).
    • Habits: early bird (早起的人), night owl (夜猫子), routine (日常), punctual (准时的).

    🗣️ Grammar & Pronunciation

    • Present Simple & Frequency: Use adverbs of frequency correctly to describe your routine (e.g., I rarely stay up late, I frequently study).
    • Compound Noun Stress: Stress the first word in compound nouns (e.g., 'timetable, 'lifestyle, 'homework).

    🎯 Exam Technique: Sequence Markers

    When describing a routine, use linking words to make your answer flow smoothly: First of all, subsequently, after that, finally.

    📝 Model Answers: Daily Routine

    Part 1: What is your typical morning routine? Are you an early bird or a night owl?

    "I am definitely an early bird. I usually wake up around six in the morning, briefly review my school notes, and pack my backpack before rushing to catch the school bus. I prefer getting things done early when my mind is fresh."

    Part 2: Describe a time when you were extremely busy.

    "I'd like to talk about last month when I was preparing for my final exams while also playing in the school basketball tournament. My schedule was incredibly hectic. I had to prioritize my tasks strictly, studying during my lunch breaks and training in the evenings. It was exhausting, but it taught me a valuable lesson about avoiding procrastination and managing my downtime effectively."

    Part 3: Is it important for teenagers to have a strict daily schedule? Has modern life become too fast-paced?

    "While prioritizing tasks is important to avoid procrastinating on homework, I believe teenagers also need sufficient downtime to recharge their batteries. Modern life has undoubtedly become extremely fast-paced, and this constant pressure to be productive can lead to severe stress and burnout among students."

    🚀 Future Goals Vocabulary

    • Career & Education: pursue a career (追求职业), major in (主修), prestigious (有声望的), scholarship (奖学金).
    • Ambition: stepping stone (垫脚石), long-term goal (长期目标), passionate about (对...充满热情), overcome obstacles (克服障碍).

    🗣️ Grammar & Pronunciation

    • Future Tenses: Use Future Continuous (I will be studying) or Future Perfect (By the time I'm 25, I will have graduated) to show advanced grammatical range.
    • Chunking: Group words into meaningful chunks and pause slightly between them to sound more confident and natural when explaining complex future plans.

    🎯 Exam Technique: Showing Ambition

    Examiners reward enthusiasm. Use strong verbs and passionate adjectives when describing your goals to show you are highly motivated.

    📝 Model Answers: Future Goals

    Part 1: What do you want to study in the future? What is your dream job?

    "I am extremely passionate about environmental science, so I plan to major in that when I go to university. Ultimately, my dream is to pursue a career as a wildlife conservationist."

    Part 2: Describe a goal you want to achieve in the next five years.

    "My main long-term goal is to secure a scholarship and get accepted into a prestigious university overseas. To achieve this, I have to maintain excellent grades in my high school classes and participate in extracurricular volunteer work. I know it will require a lot of dedication and late nights of studying, but I view it as a crucial stepping stone toward building a successful and independent future."

    Part 3: How do schools help students prepare for their careers? Is it better to have clear goals or be flexible?

    "High schools provide essential foundational knowledge, but I think they should focus more on practical skills and career counseling to help students discover what they are truly passionate about. While having clear long-term goals is vital for motivation, students must also remain flexible, because the job market is changing rapidly due to artificial intelligence."

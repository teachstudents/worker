📝 Practice Q&A: Family Matters

Part 1 Prompts

  • Tell me something about your family.
  • What do you like doing most with your family?
  • Who are you close to in your family?
  • In what way is your family important to you?

Part 2 Task Card

Describe a close friend from high school. Say: how long you've known them, how you met, what kind of person they are, and why you like them.

Model Answer (3-Minute Full Response):

"I’d like to talk about my close friend, Daniel, someone I’ve known for almost fifteen years. We first met when we were in middle school. At that time, we were both quite ambitious and joined the same English club, so we naturally spent a lot of time together. What really brought us closer was a group project where we had to design a small magazine. I remember staying up late at his house, drinking cheap soda, and laughing at our terrible drawings. Since then, we’ve stayed in touch and our friendship has grown stronger year by year.

In terms of personality, Daniel is honestly one of the most reliable people I’ve ever met. If he promises to do something, he will definitely follow through. He’s also extremely hard‑working; even when we were teenagers, he was the kind of person who would finish his homework before playing games. At the same time, he’s very open‑minded, which makes him easy to talk to. I can share any idea with him—even strange or unpopular ones—and he never judges me. Actually, he’s the opposite of judgemental; he listens first and gives thoughtful advice later.

Another thing I appreciate is that he’s quite outgoing and sociable. I’m more introverted, so he often encourages me to meet new people or try new activities. Sometimes he can be a bit nosy, especially when he thinks I’m hiding something or feeling stressed, but I know it comes from a place of care. He’s also surprisingly creative. Whenever we work on a project together, he comes up with clever ideas that I would never think of.

Of course, he isn’t perfect. He can be blunt at times, and occasionally impatient, especially when things move too slowly. And he’s definitely a little stingy—he hates spending money on anything unnecessary. But honestly, these flaws make him more human, and sometimes they even make our conversations funnier.

I like him mainly because he’s self‑assured without being arrogant. He knows who he is and what he wants, and that confidence inspires me. Whenever I face a difficult decision, he’s the first person I call. He gives honest advice, supports me when I fail, and celebrates with me when things go well. Over the years, he has become more than just a friend; he’s someone I truly trust and admire.

So overall, Daniel is a close friend I deeply value, not only because of his personality, but because of the long history and shared experiences that connect us."

🎭 Expressing Enthusiasm & Opinions

Intonation & Body Language Guide

  • Voice: Emphasize stressed syllables, vary pitch dramatically.
  • Body: Sit forward, maintain eye contact, use expressive gestures.
  • Phrases: "It was breathtaking!", "The crowd went wild!", "I'm highly sceptical of the idea that..."

    🔊 Word Stress Rules

    Rule 1: Nouns/Adj

    Stress on first syllable: leader, desert, boring

    Rule 2: Noun/Verb Shift

    Noun: progress → Verb: progress

    Rule 3: -tion/-sion

    Penultimate stress: production, recognition

    📝 Answer Length Strategy: The "Goldilocks" Rule

    Too Short

    "I work."

    Does not demonstrate range.

    Too Long

    2-min monologue on Part 1

    Derails topic & sounds memorized.

    Just Right

    Direct answer + reason/example

    "I chose it because French was my best subject..."

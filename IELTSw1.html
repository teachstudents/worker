Blueprint & Brainstorming Guide

🧠 Brainstorming and Outlining MUST come before writing the first paragraph.

Starting to write your introduction immediately is one of the most common reasons students get stuck at Band 5 or 6. Here is the evidence-based breakdown of why planning first is critical for a Band 7+.

OrderActionTime AllocationWhy?
1️⃣Analyze & Brainstorm3-5 minutesEnsures you address Task Response (all parts of the question).
2️⃣Outline Structure2-3 minutesEnsures Coherence & Cohesion (logical flow of ideas).
3️⃣Write Introduction5 minutesNow you know exactly what you are introducing.
4️⃣Write Body Paragraphs25-30 minutesYou already have the ideas ready; no writer's block.
5️⃣Write Conclusion5 minutesSummarize what you actually wrote.
6️⃣Review & Edit5 minutesCheck for grammar/spelling errors.

🏆 Why Brainstorming/Outlining Wins

1. Protects Your Task Response (TR) Score

  • Risk of Writing First: You might start writing and realize halfway through Body Paragraph 2 that you haven't addressed one part of the prompt.
  • Benefit of Planning: You check the question requirements before you commit to sentences. (e.g., If asked for causes AND solutions, your outline guarantees both are included).

2. Secures Your Coherence & Cohesion (CC) Score

  • Risk of Writing First: Ideas often become repetitive or disorganized. You might put a conclusion idea in Body 1.
  • Benefit of Planning: You arrange ideas logically (Strongest argument → Weakest argument). Examiners look for a "clear overall progression."

⚠️ What Happens If You Write the First Paragraph First?

SymptomCauseBand Impact
Incomplete EssaySpent 10 mins perfecting intro, ran out of time for Body 2.Task Response ↓ (Penalty for underlength)
Off-Topic IdeasStarted writing before fully understanding the prompt.Task Response ↓ (Irrelevant content)
Repetitive ArgumentsDidn't plan distinct ideas for each paragraph.Coherence ↓ (Lack of progression)

Pro Tip: The "Introduction Last" Strategy

Some Band 8+ writers actually write the Introduction AFTER the Body Paragraphs. Why? Sometimes your argument evolves slightly as you write. Writing the intro last ensures it perfectly matches what you actually argued in the body. Note: If you are below Band 7, stick to the standard order, but plan it all first!

🎨 Visual Brainstorming Scaffolds

Before writing any sentences, dump your ideas onto paper using one of these visual formats (taking no more than 3-5 minutes).

1. The "T-Chart" (Best for Adv/Disadv or Discuss Both Views)

Draw a simple table with two columns. This forces you to balance your argument before you start writing.

Topic: Tourism in local areas
Left Column (Benefits / View 1)Right Column (Drawbacks / View 2)

- Improves local economy
- Creates jobs for youth		 - Increases house prices
- Damages local environment
- Makes area overdeveloped

2. The "Mind Map" (Best for Causes & Effects or Problem & Solution)

Draw a circle in the center with the main topic. Draw two main branches coming off it.

🏗️ The IELTS Essay Blueprints

Once ideas are brainstormed, they must be slotted into a rigid outline. Choose the outline that matches the prompt type. A Task 2 essay should be at least 250 words and take 40 minutes.

Blueprint A: The Opinion Scaffold (Agree / Disagree)

1. Introduction (~50 words)

Introduce topic. State clear opinion (e.g., "I completely agree because...").

2. Body Paragraph 1: Reason 1 (~80 words)

State first reason. Provide supporting evidence and a real-world example.

3. Body Paragraph 2: Reason 2 (~80 words)

State second reason. Provide supporting evidence and a real-world example.

4. Conclusion (~40 words)

Summarize reasons and restate final opinion.

Blueprint B: Contrasting Viewpoints (Discuss Both Sides)

1. Introduction (~50 words)

Introduce topic. Acknowledge valid arguments on both sides.

2. Body Paragraph 1: The Other Side (~80 words)

Discuss the viewpoint you do not strongly agree with. List its benefits.

3. Body Paragraph 2: Your Side (~80 words)

Discuss the viewpoint you do agree with. Show why this side is stronger.

4. Conclusion (~40 words)

State final, definitive opinion based on the contrast.

Blueprint C: Problem & Solution Scaffold

1. Introduction (~50 words)

General statement introducing the problem. Thesis: issue can be solved via specific actions.

2. Body Paragraph 1: Causes/Problems (~80 words)

Outline the main causes or detail the specific problems occurring.

3. Body Paragraph 2: Solutions/Effects (~80 words)

Introduce proposed solutions and explain how they would work.

4. Conclusion (~40 words)

Brief summary of the proposed solutions.

📊 Academic Task 1: The 3-Paragraph Report

Approach it like a "data reporter", not a creative writer (150 words, 20 mins).

Paragraph 1: The Anchor (Paraphrase)

Answer Who, What, Where, When by replacing prompt words with synonyms.

Paragraph 2: The Overview

State the general "big picture" trend without quoting any specific numbers.

Paragraph 3: Detailed Analysis

Break down specific figures, comparisons, and trends to prove your overview.

🛡️ IELTS Academic Hedging

Why use Hedging in IELTS?

In IELTS Academic Writing (Band 7+), you should avoid making absolute claims (e.g., "Smartphones ruin teenagers' lives"). Instead, you must use "hedging" language to soften your claims, making them more objective and academic (e.g., "Smartphones can potentially have a negative impact on teenagers").

  • Demonstrates control of complex grammar (GRA).
  • Shows a sophisticated academic tone.
  • Prevents making over-generalizations in Task 2.

Examples in Task 2:

  • 🔴 Absolute (Band 5): Computers cause social isolation.
  • 🟢 Hedged (Band 7): Excessive use of computers can lead to social isolation.
  • 🌟 Hedged (Band 8): It might be argued that computers would inevitably reduce face-to-face interaction.

Hedging Reference Chart

StrategyWords to useExample
Modal Verbscan, could, may, mightThis policy might encourage students to study harder.
Adverbs of frequencyoften, usually, rarelyTeenagers often struggle with time management.
Adverbs of probabilitypossibly, likelyIt is highly likely that the trend will continue.

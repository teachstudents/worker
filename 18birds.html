🦜 Birds of Paradise

The Berlin Zoo Pitch - Simulation

The Situation: You are in a meeting room in Berlin. Your goal is to secure funding for a protected nature reserve in New Guinea and an interactive zoo exhibit.

🎭 The Setup

Every group consists of three people.

You must decide among yourselves who will take each role, just like dividing up tasks for a school presentation.

(Auto-advances when audio finishes)

🇩🇪 The German Zoo Director

The Decision-Maker
  • Speaks mostly German and very basic English.
  • Holds the money and the power.
  • Action: Interrupt if you don't understand! Ask tough questions.

🪶 The Field Expert

The Visual Storyteller
  • Knows exactly how the bird behaves and dances.
  • Action: Rely on physical action! Act out the bird's unique dance. Communicate through movement!

🤝 The Conservationist

The Language Mediator
  • The bridge between the Expert and the Director.
  • Action: Translate complex actions into simple English. Point to the expert and explain what they are doing. Ask for money!

👥 Find Your Group!

Move to your groups immediately and choose your 3 roles!

⏳ Prep Phase 1: Rubric Review (3 Mins)

Review the KASEC rubric. Plan how to maximize your points before building your pitch.
K - Knowledge (5pts) Expertly demonstrates the specific courtship dance. Explains lack of predators. Integrates indigenous cultural context naturally.
A - Application (5pts) Brilliantly articulates environmental threats (logging/farming). Proposes realistic plan advancing SDG 15 (Life on Land).
S - Skills (5pts) Expertly utilizes "Analogizing" (e.g. moves like a butterfly). Powerfully employs physical acting and props to communicate easily.
E - Ethics (5pts) Grounded in Humanistic Knowledge. Respects the "life force". Advocates for the bird's intrinsic value as an evolutionary masterpiece.
C - Communication (5pts) Masterfully simplifies scientific English for the German Director. Director actively demands clarification. Unscripted negotiation handled seamlessly.

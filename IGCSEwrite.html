IGCSE 0511 Marking Criteria Guide

Cambridge examiners grade Writing (Exercises 5, 6, and 7) based on two distinct categories: Content and Language.

🎯 Content (Relevance & Development) ✍️ Language (Range & Accuracy)
Top Band Qualities:
  • All bullet points/prompts are fully addressed and well-developed.
  • The register (formal/informal) is consistently perfectly suited to the target audience.
  • Original ideas are introduced logically (especially for Extended tier).
  • The text holds the reader's interest throughout.
 Top Band Qualities:
  • Wide range of common and less common vocabulary used appropriately.
  • Confident use of complex grammatical structures (conditionals, relative clauses, passives).
  • High level of accuracy (only minor, non-impeding 'slips').
  • Excellent cohesion using a variety of linking words and logical paragraphing.
Middle Band Qualities:
  • Most bullet points are addressed, but some may lack detail.
  • Register is generally appropriate but may slip occasionally.
  • Ideas are relevant but might rely heavily on the provided prompts rather than original thought.
 Middle Band Qualities:
  • Good control of simple structures, but complex structures contain errors.
  • Vocabulary is adequate but simple/repetitive.
  • Basic linking words (and, but, so, because) are used, but advanced cohesion is missing.

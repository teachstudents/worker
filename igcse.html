Lesson 1.1: Reading & Vocabulary Integration

Duration: 40 minutes

Lesson Objective:
Students will identify the main idea and three supporting details from a school-life blog post (AO R1) and accurately use five new school-related vocabulary words in spoken sentences (AO S1), achieving 80% accuracy on exit task.
Materials Needed:
  • Anchor text: "A Day in My School Life" blog post (simplified, with visuals)
  • Bilingual glossary (English-Chinese) for target vocabulary
  • Visual schedule with icons (UDL representation support)
  • Digital flashcards (Quizlet) for vocabulary practice
  • Exit ticket slips

Lesson Sequence

0-5 min

Hook: ARCS Attention & Relevance

Activity: Show photos of local Chinese school scenes. Prompt: "What do you see? What time does your school day start?" Students share in pairs, then 3 volunteers share with class.

5-15 min

Guided Reading: R1 Skill Building

Steps:

  1. Pre-teach 5 key vocabulary words using bilingual glossary + images
  2. Teacher reads blog post aloud while students follow
  3. Students highlight: (a) main idea sentence, (b) three time-related details
  4. Think-Pair-Share: "What is the main idea? What are three things the writer does at school?"
15-25 min

Vocabulary Application: Integrated Speaking

Activities: Digital flashcard practice (Quizlet) & "Vocabulary Chain" game.

25-35 min

Grammar Spotlight: Present Simple Introduction

Steps: Guided discovery from blog sentences -> Elicit rule -> Controlled practice.

35-40 min

Formative Assessment: Exit Ticket

Write main idea, use 2 vocab words, circle correct verb.

⚠️ Examiner Error Prevention Strategy
Target Error: Copying text verbatim instead of paraphrasing.
Prevention Protocol: Explicitly model paraphrasing: "The text says 'I attend math class at 9 a.m.' → We can say 'The writer has math at 9'." Provide sentence frames.
📝 Homework/Extension: Complete digital vocabulary quiz; write 3 original sentences.

Duration: 40 minutes | Theme: Food & Culture

Lesson Objective:
Students will identify explicit details and distinguish factual information from opinions in a simplified restaurant review (AOs R1, R3), and accurately use six sensory/evaluative vocabulary words in context to form comparative statements (AO W3), achieving 80% accuracy on exit task.
Materials Needed:
  • Anchor text: "Dragon Pearl Restaurant: A Taste of Tradition" (simplified review, 150 words)
  • Bilingual glossary: Sensory adjectives & evaluative phrases (English-Chinese)
  • Fact vs. Opinion sorting cards (laminated for reuse)
  • Graphic organizer: Dish Name | Verifiable Fact | Subjective Opinion | Sensory Detail
  • Exit ticket slips with sentence frames

Lesson Sequence

0-5 min

Hook: ARCS Attention & Cultural Relevance

Activity: Display high-quality images of local and international dishes. Prompt: "Which dish would you recommend to a visitor? Why?" Students share in pairs using frame: "I recommend ___ because it is ___."

AO Alignment: S3 (develop responses), W1 (communicate ideas)

Pedagogical Strategy: ARCS personal/cultural relevance builds engagement

5-18 min

Guided Reading: R1/R3 Skill Building with Fact/Opinion Focus

Steps:

  1. Pre-teach 6 target words using bilingual glossary + images: savory, aromatic, crispy, authentic, overpriced, highly recommend
  2. Teacher reads review aloud while students follow; highlight facts (blue) and opinions (red).
  3. Students complete graphic organizer.
  4. Think-Pair-Share: "What are two facts? What is the writer's opinion?"

AO Alignment: R1 (explicit meanings), R3 (select details for purpose)

18-30 min

Vocabulary & Grammar Integration: Comparatives

Activities:

  1. Introduce comparative structures: "more flavorful than," "crispier than."
  2. Rule presentation: short adj + -er / long adj + more + than; irregulars (good→better, bad→worse).
  3. "Taste Test Chain" speaking game.

AO Alignment: W3 (grammatical range & vocabulary)

30-37 min

Register Awareness: Formal vs. Informal Review Language

Steps: "The food was super good lol" vs. "The dish was highly recommended." Guided discussion on IGCSE appropriate register.

AO Alignment: W4 (appropriate register for purpose/audience)

37-40 min

Formative Assessment: Exit Ticket

Write one fact, one opinion, and create a comparative sentence.

⚠️ Examiner Error Prevention Strategy
Target Error: Confusing facts with opinions; using informal/text-speak register.
Prevention Protocol: Teach "Fact = Verifiable / Opinion = Judgment + Evidence" matrix. Provide formal register checklist.
📝 Homework/Extension: Complete digital vocabulary & comparative quiz.

Lesson 4.1: Reading & Vocabulary Integration

Duration: 40 minutes | Theme: Hobbies & Interests

Lesson Objective:
Students will scan an interview transcript to locate specific information about schedules and achievements (AOs R1, R3) and accurately use hobby-related verbs with present continuous tense and correctly placed frequency adverbs (AO W3), achieving 80% accuracy on exit task.
Materials Needed:
  • Anchor text: "Young Athlete Spotlight: Balancing Training & Studies" (interview transcript, 180 words)
  • Bilingual glossary: Hobby verbs & frequency adverbs (English-Chinese)
  • Scanning task cards with time/activity/achievement columns
  • Frequency adverb placement ruler visual (Never → Rarely → Sometimes → Often → Always)
  • Exit ticket slips with sentence transformation frames

Lesson Sequence

0-5 min

Hook: ARCS Attention & Personal Relevance

Activity: Display collage of diverse hobbies (e-sports, traditional dance, robotics, painting, basketball). Prompt: "What do you do in your free time? How often?" Students share in pairs using frame: "I ___ ___ times a week."

AO Alignment: S1 (communicate ideas), W3 (vocabulary/grammar control)

Pedagogical Strategy: ARCS personal relevance builds engagement; Bandura low-stakes sharing builds confidence

Differentiation: UDL visual/kinesthetic prompts; DI allow L1 brainstorming before structured English output

5-18 min

Guided Reading: R1/R3 Skill Building with Scanning Focus

Steps:

  1. Pre-teach 6 target words: practice, compete, collect, perform, schedule, dedicate
  2. Introduce scanning strategy: "Don't read every word. Look for numbers, times, proper nouns, and activity verbs."
  3. Students complete scanning task: extract training times, competition dates, and personal achievements from interview
  4. Think-Pair-Share: "What is the interviewee's main hobby? When do they train? What is their goal?"

AO Alignment: R1 (specific information), R3 (select details for purpose)

Scaffolding: Scanning protocol checklist; highlighter strategy for keywords; timed practice builds exam stamina

Differentiation: Tiered scanning: (a) Pre-circled keywords to locate details, (b) Guided column completion, (c) Independent extraction with justification

18-30 min

Vocabulary & Grammar Integration: Present Continuous & Adverb Placement

Activities:

  1. Introduce present continuous: am/is/are + -ing for current/temporary actions & future arrangements
  2. Contrast with present simple: permanent habits vs. current focus ("I play football" vs. "I am practicing for the tournament")
  3. Frequency adverb placement rule: Before main verb, AFTER "be" verb (I always practice / I am always practicing)
  4. Controlled practice: Sentence transformation worksheet + "Adverb Placement" card sort activity

AO Alignment: W3 (grammatical range & accuracy)

Explicit instruction: Contextualizes tense contrast within authentic interview genre

TPACK: Interactive whiteboard drag-and-drop for adverb placement practice

30-37 min

Application: Timed Detail Extraction & Sentence Formation

Steps:

  1. Students scan a short new text (45 seconds) to find 3 hobby-related details
  2. Using extracted details, form present continuous sentences with correct adverb placement
  3. Peer check: Verify tense accuracy & adverb position using placement ruler

AO Alignment: R3 (detail selection), W3 (grammar application)

Genre-based instruction: Explicit teaching of information transfer (reading → writing) skill

37-40 min

Formative Assessment: Exit Ticket

Tasks:

  1. Scan & locate: What time does the athlete practice?
  2. Transform to present continuous: "She trains every day." → "She ___ (train) for the competition this month."
  3. Correct placement: "I am practicing often." / "I often am practicing." → Circle correct version

AO Alignment: R1, R3, W3

Differentiation: Exit ticket includes sentence frames & visual placement cue; allow verbal response via Flipgrid

⚠️ Examiner Error Prevention Strategy
Target Error: Misplacing frequency adverbs after -ing verbs; overusing present continuous for permanent habits; failing to scan efficiently under time pressure (common R3/W3 weaknesses per Examiner Reports)
Prevention Protocol: Teach "BE Rule": Frequency adverb goes AFTER "am/is/are". Display visual placement ruler prominently. Implement "Scan First, Read Second" protocol with timer to build exam pacing. Explicitly contrast stative vs. dynamic verbs early.
📝 Homework/Extension:
Core: Complete digital present continuous & adverb placement quiz; practice scanning 2 short hobby articles
Extension: Write 3 sentences about a friend's current hobby using present continuous + frequency adverb
Support: Review bilingual glossary; practice adverb placement with sentence strip manipulatives

Duration: 40 minutes | Theme: Hobbies & Interests

Lesson Objective:
Students will extract specific tips and identify speaker attitudes from a hobby podcast (AOs L1, L2) and deliver a structured 2-minute presentation about a personal hobby using visual aids, clear organization, and interactive Q&A (AOs S1, S3, S4).
Materials Needed:
  • Audio podcast: "Top 3 Tips for Practicing Your Hobby" (90 seconds, clear pronunciation, structured advice)
  • Listening worksheet: Tip Number | Action Verb | Why It Works / Speaker Attitude
  • Speaking cue cards: Presentation structure (Intro → 3 Points → Conclusion → Q&A), sentence frames, transition phrases
  • Recording devices & visual aid templates (simple poster/digital slide)
  • Self-assessment checklist aligned to S1, S3, S4 rubric criteria

Lesson Sequence

0-5 min

Warm-up: Presentation Structure & Pronunciation Activation

Activity: Teacher models 3 opening lines with varying clarity/pace. Students identify best version & repeat key transition phrases ("Firstly," "Another important point is," "To conclude"). Focus on sentence stress & clear enunciation.

AO Alignment: S4 (pronunciation), S1 (communicate ideas)

Retrieval practice: Strengthens prosodic awareness & structural awareness before speaking task

5-20 min

Listening Skill Building: L1/L2 Strategy Instruction

Steps:

  1. Pre-listening: Predict 3 tips based on title; activate schema about skill improvement
  2. First listen: Gist & attitude task – "What is the main message? Is the speaker encouraging/cautional/enthusiastic?"
  3. Second listen: Specific information task – Complete table: Tip # | Action | Reason/Attitude Marker
  4. Post-listening: Compare answers; teacher reviews transcript, highlighting signposting language & attitude vocabulary (L2 focus)

AO Alignment: L1 (specific information), L2 (ideas/opinions/attitudes)

Strategy instruction: Explicit teaching of "listen for signposts & evaluative language" protocol

20-35 min

Speaking Practice: Structured Presentation & Q&A

Steps:

  1. Teacher models presentation using cue card & simple visual: "I'm here to talk about ___. First, I practice by ___. Another reason I enjoy it is ___. To conclude..."
  2. Teach response expansion & interaction formula (S3): Statement + Example + Question to audience ("For example, I ___ . Have you ever tried ___?")

Activity 3: Contrast Connectors & Complex Syntax

Rules:

✍️ Lesson 7.2: Grammar & Writing – Balanced Opinion Paragraph

Activity 1: Cohesion & Tone Detective (6 minutes)

Look at these responses to "`Discuss` whether technology benefits students":

  1. ✓ "While digital tools offer connectivity, excessive usage disrupts focus. Nevertheless, with moderation, they enhance learning outcomes." (Cohesive, balanced, formal)
  2. ✗ "Phones are good. But computers are bad. I use iPad a lot." (List-like, informal)
  3. ✗ "Despite it helps us learn, it also makes us lazy." (Syntax error + informal)

Discuss: Which meets `Discuss` criteria? Why does academic argument need contrast linkers + formal register?

Activity 2: Grammar Practice – Complex Contrast Structures

Rules:

  • Although/While + subject + verb
  • Despite/In spite of + noun / gerund (-ing)
  • Nevertheless/Conversely → start new sentence, comma after

2. Section Breakdown (1-4)

Section 1: Social/Transactional Cam 5 Cam 17

Context: Conversation between two speakers (e.g., booking, enquiry, registration).

Target Score: 8-10/10 correct (Band 6.5-7.0 baseline).

Common Question Types

  • Form Completion (Names, Numbers, Dates)
  • Note Completion
  • Table Completion
Top Trap: Self-Correction (D1)
Example (Cam 5 Test 1): Caller gives phone number, corrects digits immediately.
Example (Practice Test 202): "07942 815... actually 07942 851 367".
Strategy: Wait for the speaker to finish the thought. Do not write until confirmed.
Cambridge Evolution: Cambridge 17 shows higher density of number traps (prices, dates) compared to Cambridge 5.

Section 2: Social/Monologue Cam 6 Cam 8

Context: One speaker (e.g., tour guide, announcement, presentation).

Target Score: 7-9/10 correct.

Common Question Types

  • Map/Plan Labeling
  • Multiple Choice
  • Diagram Labeling
Top Trap: Spatial Confusion & Red Herrings (D2)
Example (Cam 6 Test 1): Map labeling where locations are mentioned but changed.
Example (Practice Test v1): "We planned to put the café here, but moved it there."
Strategy: Orient yourself (mark Entrance/North). Listen for final decisions, not past plans.

Section 3: Educational Discussion Cam 8 Cam 17

Context: 2-4 speakers (e.g., students + tutor discussing a project).

Target Score: 6-8/10 correct.

Common Question Types

  • Matching (Opinions to Speakers)
  • Multiple Choice
  • Classification
Top Trap: Speaker Tracking & Paraphrase (D3)
Example (Cam 8 Test 1): Multiple students express opinions; question asks for specific person.
Example (Practice Test v3): Question: "job" → Audio: "occupation".
Strategy: Note initials (A, B, C) next to opinions. Focus on MEANING, not exact words.

Section 4: Academic Lecture Cam 5 Cam 17

Context: One speaker (e.g., university lecture).

Target Score: 5-7/10 correct.

Common Question Types

  • Sentence Completion
  • Summary Completion
  • Short Answer
Top Trap: Vocabulary Gap & Concentration Drop (V1)
Example (Cam 17 Test 4): Technical terms used without definition.
Example (Practice Test v2): Answer spoken in final 30 seconds.
Strategy: Use context clues. Stay focused until the audio completely ends.

3. Universal Trap Database

Based on analysis of Cambridge 5, 6, 8, 17 and Practice Tests v1-v5, 202.

3.1 The 5 Major Distractor Types

CodeTrap NameAudio SignalStrategy
D1 Self-Correction "Sorry", "Actually", "I mean", "Wait" Wait for final confirmed information
D2 Red Herring "Or", "Alternatively", "You could" Identify what was actually chosen
D3 Paraphrase Synonyms, rephrased concepts Focus on meaning, not exact words
N1 Number Trap Stress patterns (-teen vs -ty) Listen to syllable stress carefully
D4 False Start "I was going to", "Initially" Ignore abandoned ideas

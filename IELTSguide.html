Listening Modules 4 Sections

MODULE 1: Listening Section 1 (Social/Transactional)

Tag: #module_listening_1 | Difficulty: ⭐ Foundation

  • Context: Social/Transactional (booking, enquiry, registration)
  • Speakers: 2 speakers (customer + service provider)
  • Question Types: Form completion, note completion, table completion
  • Target Score: 8-10/10 correct

Common Traps & Distractors

S1-D1: Self-Correction
Audio: "It's on Monday — oh no, sorry, it's actually Tuesday."
Strategy: Wait for final confirmed information.
S1-D2: Number Confusion
Audio: "13 vs 30", "14 vs 40"
Strategy: Listen to stress patterns (thirTEEN vs THIRty).
S1-D3: Spelling Trap
Audio: Words spelled out letter-by-letter.
Strategy: Practice common IELTS spellings (double letters).

Teaching Strategies

  • Prediction: Identify answer type before listening (Name? Number?).
  • Number Dictation: Drill 13/30, 14/40 discrimination.
  • Distractor Awareness: "Spot the change" listening clips.

MODULE 2: Listening Section 2 (Social/Monologue)

Tag: #module_listening_2 | Difficulty: ⭐⭐ Intermediate

  • Context: Social/Monologue (tour guide, announcement)
  • Speakers: 1 speaker
  • Question Types: Map/plan labeling, multiple choice
  • Target Score: 7-9/10 correct

Common Traps & Distractors

S2-D1: Spatial Confusion
Audio: "On your left" vs "On the north side"
Strategy: Orient yourself; mark entrance first.
S2-D2: Past Plan Trap
Audio: "We planned to... but moved it"
Strategy: Listen for current vs. past tense.

Teaching Strategies

  • Spatial Language: Teach left/right, opposite, adjacent, corner.
  • Map Analysis: Predict what might be labeled before audio starts.
  • MC Strategy: Elimination technique practice.

MODULE 3: Listening Section 3 (Educational Discussion)

Tag: #module_listening_3 | Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ Upper-Intermediate

  • Context: Educational/Training (academic discussion)
  • Speakers: 2-4 speakers (students + tutor)
  • Question Types: Matching, multiple choice, classification
  • Target Score: 6-8/10 correct

Common Traps & Distractors

S3-D1: Speaker Tracking
Audio: Multiple speakers talking rapidly.
Strategy: Note initials (A, B, C) next to opinions.
S3-D2: Paraphrase Miss
Audio: Q: "job" → Audio: "occupation"
Strategy: Focus on meaning, not words.

Teaching Strategies

  • Speaker Tracking: Use tracking sheets during practice.
  • Paraphrase: Analyze synonym chains (Question → Audio → Answer).
  • Matching: Relationship mapping drills.

MODULE 4: Listening Section 4 (Academic Lecture)

Tag: #module_listening_4 | Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ Advanced

  • Context: Academic Lecture (university presentation)
  • Speakers: 1 speaker (lecturer)
  • Question Types: Sentence completion, summary completion
  • Target Score: 5-7/10 correct

Common Traps & Distractors

S4-D1: Vocabulary Gap
Audio: Unknown academic terms.
Strategy: Use context clues; don't freeze.
S4-D2: Signpost Miss
Audio: Moving to next point without pause.
Strategy: Follow discourse markers ("Moving on to...", "The key point is...").

Teaching Strategies

  • Structure: Teach lecture structure: intro → background → points → conclusion.
  • Signposting: Identify signpost language lists.
  • Note-Taking: Create personal abbreviation systems.

Speaking Modules 3 Parts

MODULE 5: Speaking Part 1 (Introduction & Interview)

Tag: #module_speaking_1 | Duration: 4-5 minutes

  • Focus: Personal/familiar topics (home, work, studies)
  • Questions: 10-12 questions across 2-3 topics
  • Answer Length: 2-4 sentences per question (Band 7+)

Common Traps

SP1-T1: Short Answers
Error: 1 sentence only (Band 5).
Strategy: Use WHY + EXAMPLE technique.
SP1-T2: Over-Rehearsal
Error: Sounds memorized.
Strategy: Keep answers natural and varied.

Teaching Strategies

  • Extension: Teach "Because...", "For example...", "Especially...".
  • Fluency: Quick-fire Q&A drills.

MODULE 6: Speaking Part 2 (Long Turn)

Tag: #module_speaking_2 | Duration: 3-4 minutes

  • Focus: Extended monologue on given topic
  • Format: Cue card (1 min prep + 1-2 min talk)
  • Answer Length: 1.5-2 minutes continuous speech

Common Traps

SP2-T1: Under-Time
Error: Speaking <1 minute.
Strategy: Practice to 1:30-2:00 consistently.
SP2-T2: Bullet Miss
Error: Not covering all cue card points.
Strategy: Check off each bullet during speech.

Teaching Strategies

  • Structure: PPF Framework (Past-Present-Future).
  • Preparation: 1-minute planning strategy (Keywords only).

MODULE 7: Speaking Part 3 (Two-Way Discussion)

Tag: #module_speaking_3 | Duration: 4-5 minutes

  • Focus: Abstract/analytical discussion
  • Questions: 4-6 questions (deeper exploration)
  • Answer Length: 30-60 seconds per question

Common Traps

SP3-T1: Personal Only
Error: Only personal examples.
Strategy: Use general/societal examples.
SP3-T2: No Depth
Error: Superficial answers.
Strategy: Use P.E.E. (Point-Explain-Example).

Teaching Strategies

  • Abstract Thinking: Discuss societal issues, not just personal ones.
  • Speculation: Practice "might", "could", "likely".

Speaking Band Descriptors (Summary)

View Full Criteria (FC, LR, GRA, P)

Fluency & Coherence

Band 7: Speaks at length without noticeable effort; uses range of connectives.
Band 6: Willing to speak at length; some hesitation; uses simple connectives.

Lexical Resource

Band 7: Flexible enough to discuss variety of topics; uses some less common vocabulary.
Band 6: Adequate for familiar topics; attempts paraphrasing.

Grammatical Range & Accuracy

Band 7: Range of structures including some complex forms; frequent error-free sentences.
Band 6: Mix of simple and complex structures; some errors in complex structures.

Pronunciation

Band 7: Shows all features with some control; generally easy to understand.
Band 6: Uses some features effectively; generally intelligible.

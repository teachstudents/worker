Part 1: What is Modal Backshift?

Rule Introduction

Modal Verb Backshift is used when we report what someone said in the past. When the main reporting verb is in the past tense, the modal verbs inside the reported clause must change to reflect the shift back in time.

Why is this important for Writing?

  • Appears in narrative writing prompts like diary entries and speech reports.
  • Shows examiners you can handle complex reported speech.
  • Earns points in the Grammatical Accuracy category.
  • Demonstrates discourse processing fluency.

Where You Will Use This in Writing:

Common Writing Formats:

  • Diary Entries: The teacher said we would participate...
  • Speech Reports: The organizer mentioned it might take two hours...
  • Letters: You said you would help me...
  • Proposals: Students said they could plant trees...

Part 2: Modal Backshift Reference Chart

Direct Speech Original Reported Speech Narrative Example
will would "I will help," she said.
→ She said she would help.
can could "I can come," he said.
→ He said he could come.
may might "I may join," they said.
→ They said they might join.
shall would or should "Shall we go?" she asked.
→ She asked if we would go.
must had to "I must study," he said.
→ He said he had to study.
could could no change "I could help," she said.
→ She said she could help.
would would no change "I would try," he said.
→ He said he would try.
should should no change "You should rest," she said.
→ She said I should rest.
might might no change "It might rain," they said.
→ They said it might rain.
need not need not no change "You need not worry," he said.
→ He said I need not worry.

Color Code Legend:

will becomes would Future to Conditional
can becomes could Ability to Past Ability
may becomes might Possibility to Past Possibility
must becomes had to Necessity to Past Necessity
no change could, would, should, might

Part 3: Correct Exam Examples

Model Sentences Green Campus Topic

1. The principal said we would receive certificates.

Direct speech: "You will receive certificates."

2. Teachers told us we had to sort waste properly.

Direct speech: "You must sort waste properly."

3. Students said they could plant trees together.

Direct speech: "We can plant trees together."

4. The organizer mentioned it might take three hours.

Direct speech: "It may take three hours."

5. Everyone agreed we should protect the environment.

Direct speech: "We should protect the environment."

Common Errors to Avoid:

Wrong: The principal said we will receive certificates.

Correct: The principal said we would receive certificates.

Wrong: Teachers told us we must to sort waste.

Correct: Teachers told us we had to sort waste.
